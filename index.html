The Architecture

Aerodynamics Meets Durability

Blended Wing Body

Traditional "tube and wing" fuselages waste space. Our BWB design integrates the cargo hold into the lifting body itself, providing 40% more internal volume than standard bush planes.

Gravel Protection

Rear-mounted pusher propellers are physically shielded from gravel spray by the broad wing body. This drastic reduction in FOD (Foreign Object Debris) saves thousands in prop maintenance on rough strips.

The Loading Ramp

No more hand-bombing boxes through a side door. Our 2-pallet wide rear ramp is designed for forklifts and drive-on cargo, accommodating snowmobiles and quads with ease.

Business Model

One Airframe.
Two Chemistries.

Aviation is seasonal. Your battery shouldn't limit where you fly. We designed the Mule to swap its core energy storage based on the mission environment.

Winter Mode (The North)

Uses Sodium-Ion packs for extreme reliability and safety. No thermal runaway risk, perfect for unattended parking in -40°C.

Summer Mode (The South)

Swap to Lithium-Ion packs for the Caribbean season. Doubles payload capacity (up to 2,200kg) when cold is not a factor.

Engineering Challenges & FAQ

Why not just pure electric?
Infrastructure. The North doesn't have 400kW chargers at every remote strip. We bring our own charger (The Power Pallet). It allows us to operate autonomously in the most remote regions on Earth.
How do you handle Balance (W&B)?
Conventional planes struggle with heavy batteries. Our BWB design allows us to place batteries in the nose to counteract the rear-mounted engines and cargo, creating a naturally stable "Dumbbell" weight distribution that improves longitudinal stability.
What about deep winter operations?
Sodium chemistry allows the plane to "hibernate" without damage. When you're ready to fly, the Power Pallet can act as a ground heater, pre-heating the battery loops and cabin for reliable starts even at -40°C.
Can the solar panels recharge the main battery?

Yes, but think "Trickle," not "Supercharge."

In ideal sunlight, the 10kW Solar Skin adds approximately 20-25% charge per day (or roughly 40 miles of range).

While it won't replace a generator for quick turnarounds, it means that if the aircraft is parked for 4-5 days, it can self-recharge to 100% completely off-grid—providing "free fuel" for your next leg.
